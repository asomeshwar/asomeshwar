About

I am a PhD Candidate in Psychology at the University of Oregon, where I am advised by Jennifer Pfeifer and Sara Weston. My research examines how adolescents and young adults navigate close relationships with parents and peers, and how everyday interpersonal dynamics shape personality development, self-esteem, and mental health over time. I am particularly interested in perception discrepancies — the gaps between how individuals see themselves and how significant others perceive them. I use multilevel modeling, structural equation modeling, network analysis, and computational text analysis.

Current Research Interests

Adolescent Relationship Dynamics

Examining how adolescents and young adults navigate close relationships with parents and peers, and how these dynamics unfold across daily life.

Ongoing

Perception Discrepancies

Investigating discrepancies between how individuals see themselves, their relationships, and their everyday experiences, and how close others perceive those same targets — and what these gaps (or lack thereof) mean for mental health and well-being.

Ongoing

Personality Development & Mental Health

Studying how everyday interpersonal dynamics shape personality development, self-esteem, and mental health trajectories over time.
